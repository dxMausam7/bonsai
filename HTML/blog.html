Technology and Its impact on Daily Lives

Introduction

Christian Lous Lange once said “Technology is a useful servant but a dangerous slave”. Technology can be either a useful tool that advances civilization or a deadly weapon that contributes to the demolition of society. Whether it was for personal or professional reasons, everyone has used technology at some point in their lives. Almost every aspect of modern life is impacted by technology, including productivity, business, socialization, food and healthcare access, and transportation efficiency and safety. The power of the internet has facilitated the emergence of global communities and made it easier to exchange information and resources.


Its Positive impact
  • Increased efficiency and productivity
  • Better communication and collaboration
  • Improved healthcare
  • Increased convenience
  • Environmental benefits
    		•


    However, excessive use of some technologies has been connected to social division, a decline in mental health, and privacy issues. The purpose of this blog is to explore both bright and dark side of using technology. We would undoubtedly notice how much technology has changed our lives if we were to actually compare how we live now with how life was a few years ago.



    In Communication

    It has given us a great deal of freedom as well as numerous opportunities to save money and time. It is impossible to credit all of this change to a single type of technology. The level of technology that a country has attained can be used to gauge how much its economy has expanded. The one piece of modern technology that has been most useful in our lives is the cell phone, which is now regarded as a symbol of civilization. Online databases, personalized web pages, discussion boards, and chat rooms are all products of the great technological revolution.

    Emails, video conferencing, and other forms of instant messaging are just a few examples of how the information technology industry has completely changed human communication. People can now build mental bridges to shorten the distance thanks to technology. Each day that goes by, more people are using the internet, and this growth is extremely rapid. More than 700 million people from all over the world are actually connected by the internet.


    In Education

    Unquestionably cutting-edge, this technology has greatly improved both the educational standards and the way we communicate. A person can obtain information on any subject from anywhere at any time thanks to this fantastic technological advancement. It has also improved on many of the methods we use to meet all of our needs and expectations. It has given us a lot simpler and quicker solutions to pressing issues.


    E-commerce

    You can check it out online right now if you want to buy anything. You don't need to go very far. We place orders and everything is delivered right to our door. Additionally, the availability of numerous cards and phone payment options has made online shopping convenient. This demonstrates how technology is affecting our daily lives.


    In Health Care

    Healthcare has been transformed by digital technology. AI, machine learning, blockchain, mobile healthcare apps, smartwatches, and a slew of other technological breakthroughs have all contributed to the advancement of healthcare. The use of technology has increased the speed and output of various business activities such as marketing, promotions, sales, and accounting. Multiple aspects of business require the use of technology today, from internal operations to the external output of activities, from minor processes to major activities.


    Conclusion

    Technology was created to serve the world, but people used it so extensively that it caused serious problems such as health issues, privacy issues, and social issues. Health issues are regarded as critical issues that affect the user's mental and physical health. Furthermore, user privacy is jeopardized as a result of spying and data theft. Furthermore, socializing is hampered by rapid mood swings and a lack of social skills. People should spend less time communicating with one another and use technology at rates that do not harm people in order to avoid future problems.
